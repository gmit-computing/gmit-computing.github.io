We are a group of individuals, mainly from the Department of Computing and Applied Physics at GMIT, who are interested in various topics related to computing and the Internet of Things.

People

Ian McLoughlin

ian.mcloughlin@gmit.ie

Dr. Ian McLoughlin is a lecturer in the Department of Computer Science and Applied Physics at the Galway-Mayo Institute of Technology (GMIT) in Ireland, where he teaches modules in Algorithms, Mobile Application Development, Graphics Programming, and Graph Theory, among others.

Previously he held academic positions at the Institute of Technology, Sligo, the Digital Enterprise Research Institute (now The Insight Centre for Data Analytics) at the National University of Ireland, Galway, and the Clique Research Cluster (now also Insight) at University College Dublin.

Ian has worked in industry at the Aon Centre for Innovation and Analytics in Dublin and at IBM Research in Zurich, Switzerland. He was involved in the development of in-house web applications at IBM Research, Aon, UCD, NUIG and Stanford. In his current role, Ian has collaborated with industry partners in a number of areas, such as Image Recognition, Cloud Computing, and Mobile Computing.

Ian holds a Ph.D. in Mathematics from the National University of Ireland, Galway for which he was awarded an IRCSET scholarship. In 2014 he was the recipient of a Fulbright TechImpact Award which he took up as a Visiting Scholar at Stanford University in California.

Interests: Combinatorics, Data Analytics, Mobile Applications, Cloud Computing, Computer Vision, Mathematics.

Seán Duignan

sean.duignan@gmit.ie

Dr. Seán Duignan is Head of Department of Computer Science and Applied Physics at the Galway-Mayo Institute of Technology. In addition to academic administration of the department, he also lectures on undergraduate and postgraduate programmes in computer science; in areas including network programming, computer forensics and research methods.

Prior to joining GMIT, Seán held a number of Software/IT consultancy positions in Boston, New York, Dublin and Galway. His industry experience focused primarily on software design and development for corporate networks, as well as network configuration, optimisation and management.

He earned his Bachelors, Masters and Ph.D at the National University of Ireland, Galway (NUIG). He also holds a Higher Diploma in Systems Analysis & Design from the University and a Higher Certificate in Teaching and Learning in Higher Education. Seán is a Fellow of the Irish Computer Society (FICS), a member of the (national) Third Level Computing Forum, and a member of the Board of ITAG - The Information Technology Association of Galway.

Seán has served as an expert evaluator at the Directorate General (Information Society and Media), European Commission, Brussels under EU Framework Programme 7 (various calls relating to e-Infrastructures for Scientific Communities; 2007 – 2013), and is a registered expert evaluator for H2020 (invited evaluator for Call H2020-EINFRA-2016 and Call H2020-INFRASUPP-2016, (June 2016)). He has also served as a reviewer/committee member on numerous international conferences, and is author or co-author of fourteen peer-reviewed publications over the last ten years.

Interests: Computer Forensics, System Security, Computer Science Pedagogy, Cloud Computing, Internet of Things.

Damien Costello

damien.costello@gmit.ie

Damien has been a lecturer with the Department of Computer Science and Applied Physics since 2000. He has lectured in many areas of software development including Mobile Applications Development, Operating Systems, Artificial Intelligence, Embedded Software Development and general programming.

Previously, he worked as a software developer in Nortel Networks developing and testing for IP Telephony based solutions. He also worked as developer in DEC.

Current research interests include the use of technology in sport for assessment, improvement and education. Gesture based computing, wearable technologies and applications of mobile and context based systems in everyday life.

Interests: Mobile Applications, Wearable Technology, Gesture Based Computing, Context Based Computing, Artificial Intelligence, Software Development.
